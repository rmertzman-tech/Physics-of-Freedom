Part 01

The Existentialist Foundation

The existentialists were right about the challenge — building a coherent self in an environment that offers no guarantee of coherence. They just didn't have the physics to make it precise. We do now.

Most people think of existentialism as a philosophy of despair — angst, meaninglessness, alienation. That reading misses its radical core. The existentialists were making a claim with enormous practical consequences: you are not born with a fixed nature. You build yourself.

Landauer's Principle: The Cost of Choosing

In 1961, physicist Rolf Landauer established that the erasure of information is a physical process that costs energy. Every time you override an impulse, update a belief, or commit to a new direction, you are performing information work. The "weight" of a difficult decision is not metaphor — it is a signature of real metabolic expenditure.

When the System Fails

Two cases. Fifty years apart. Entirely different populations. The same physics. The same ATCF collapse sequence. The same moral misdiagnosis.

Place the Rust Belt collapse and the Great Rewiring of Childhood side by side and the structural identity becomes unmistakable. Both involve a Decoherence Cascade: environmental entropy depletes T′ (biological baseline), which collapses C′ (future horizon), which eliminates F′ (the capacity for free will). Both are diagnosed as individual character failures. Both are actually system design problems.

Part 03

Individual vs. System Ethics

Traditional ethics asks what you should do. Constructor Ethics asks what kind of environment makes ethical agency physically possible — and who is responsible for building it.

The cases in Part 2 make the system level inescapable. When an entire generation develops anxiety and depression simultaneously, when workers across a whole regional economy develop "despair deaths" in parallel, the explanation cannot be individual character failure. The only explanation that fits the scale is system design.

A society is itself a Constructor — a system that either enables or prevents certain transformations. When it systematically depletes the metabolic budgets of its members, it is not a neutral background. It is an active agent of harm, regardless of intent.

Part 04

The Mandate to Build

Social contract theorists were trying to solve a problem that physics can now describe precisely. You have the physics. Now evaluate the answers.

Hobbes, Locke, Rousseau, Rawls — each trying to answer: Why should I accept constraints on my freedom, and what do I get in return? Their answers differ dramatically. But they share an assumption that Coherence Ethics makes explicit:

🔍
The Hidden Presupposition
Social Contracts Presuppose Functional ATCFs
Social contract theory assumes agents who can understand rules, project futures, weigh consequences, and sustain commitments. It presupposes agents whose ATCF is operational. What happens when the environment makes that functionality impossible for large numbers of people? That is the question classical theory was not equipped to answer — because it lacked the physics.

Key Terms for Chapter 5

These 10 terms will appear throughout Hobbes, Locke, Rousseau, Rawls, Nozick, and Nussbaum in Chapter 5. Use the search to find any term quickly.

🔍
Identity Kernel
The low-entropy structure of values, memories, and habits that gives a person coherent selfhood across time. Built from metabolic Fire; maintained as cognitive Ice. The object of protection for all social contract theory.
ATCF (A, T, C, F)
Adaptive Temporal Coherence Function: the four-variable system (Autobiographical Continuity, Biological Substrate, Future Horizon, Meta-Constructive Capacity) describing an agent's operational capacity for coherent agency across time.
Sapolsky Mask
The throttling of prefrontal capacity under chronic stress. Shifts cognitive resources from deliberation (Architect) to survival heuristics (Emergency Responder). Renders authentic ethical choice physically inaccessible. Not a moral failing — a physics event.
Decoherence Cascade
The sequential collapse of ATCF variables under environmental entropy injection. T′ depletes first, collapsing C′ (future horizon), which eliminates F′ (free will). Both Rust Belt and Great Rewiring cases follow this exact sequence.
Morphic Fidelity
The alignment between an agent's internal Identity Kernel (actual values and commitments) and their external actions. High fidelity = existentialist authenticity. Low fidelity = bad faith. Misalignment accumulates Moral Debt.
Moral Debt
Accumulated unresolved contradictions between an agent's core values and their actual behavior. Moral Debt makes the Identity Kernel metastable — brittle under pressures a coherent person would absorb easily. Not a sin: a structural condition.
Cooling Intervention
A system-level action that reduces environmental entropy, restoring the metabolic Fire necessary for cognitive and ethical work. Phone-free schools, stable housing, consistent income — these are cooling interventions, not charity.
BioBonds
Social instruments that provide thermodynamic stability — predictable income, housing, nutrition — as agency infrastructure. Reframes welfare from charity to the prerequisite for ethical agency itself. Comparable to Rawls's "primary goods," grounded in physics.
Adaptive Moral Commonwealth
A society designed to maintain coherence as its primary governance goal. Entropy-literate: treats the biological, psychological, and systemic conditions for agency as constitutional obligations. The AMC is what social contract theory is groping toward.
Constructor Theory
A physics framework asking which transformations are possible vs. impossible given available resources. Applied to ethics: moral behavior is a physical task with real resource requirements. If those resources are absent, the task is literally impossible — not just difficult.
